A Living
Contradiction

By Ryan Tucker

Hey! My name is Ryan, and I hail from the great city of Washington, D.C. I am a University student, working towards my degree in Counseling Psychology. I have 2 cats and enjoy playing video games and reading numerous types of books.

Coming out took a while for me. Growing up in a Christian home, I almost hadn’t even heard about gay people, let alone understood what my own feelings were. I finally realized I was gay when I was 15. I had a friend at the time that I was convinced was my “best friend.” I wanted to do everything with and for this person because I thought that is what best friends did. When he got a girlfriend, though, I became exceptionally jealous, but I couldn’t understand why. Shouldn’t I have been happy for him? Regardless, I soon began to realize that the reason I was so upset was because I had a crush on him.

I didn’t come to terms with that until I told my actual best friend (a female) that I was gay. I told her on April Fools day so I could back out if I needed to. She was wonderful, though, and helped me accept who I was. The path to telling my family was difficult. My dad is a chaplain (pastor) in the Air Force, so there are still issues there. For the most part, though, the people have accepted me, and now that I am older I feel comfortable with being who I am.

I am a student. I love movies. I love food. I love video games. I love reading. I love theater. Being gay is a part of who I am, but it hasn’t changed me. It doesn’t entirely define me, yet it adds a richness to other factors of my life, just as any person would say. You don’t have to fit into a mold to be considered gay or straight. Just be you, and doing so is what will bring true happiness.

Cheers!
