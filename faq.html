Find a Mentor

What do mentors do?
Mentors volunteer to help you figure out your identity and potentially come out of the closet. In addition to providing a listening ear, they draw from their own experiences to relate to and address what you would like to discuss.
How am I matched to a mentor?
We make sure mentors share your potential experiences and interests by comparing their questionnaire responses and comments to yours. We will try to give you a choice of two mentors to connect with when possible.
Who can find a mentor?
Nearly anyone. We started OutBound to make sure that people can connect with someone who has been through their situation regardless of where they live in the United States or their situation (age, gender, religion, sexual orientation, etc.) However, we are not at this time able to support people 1) under 13 years old, 2) from outside of the United States, or 3) at a high risk for suicide, violence, chronic mental illness, etc. In these situations, we recommend several, more appropriate resources.
How do I communicate with my mentor?
All communication takes place through a convenient and secure online instant messaging platform called Slack. The platform is easily accessed through a web app, email integration, and mobile apps.
Is this safe?
We make sure you're always safe by carefully screening mentors (including conducting mentor background checks and relevant training) before we ask if you would like to connect with them. We also monitor communications to make sure both you and the mentor are comfortable and safe; but don't worry, we'll never butt in!
Is this anonymous/private?
We understand privacy is important, so adapt to what works best for you. Some are comfortable with communicating through Facebook messages, whereas others create new email accounts for OutBound. We will always respect your privacy. Furthermore, questionnaire responses and communications with a mentor are never seen by anyone but an admin.

Become a Mentor

What are the responsibilities?
Mentors volunteer to help people figure out their identity and gradually come out of the closet by drawing from the experiences that they went through. Mentors are not meant to serve as therapists, but to address any potential concerns of the mentee and to provide a sympathetic listening ear.
What is the time commitment?
We encourage mentors to communicate with their mentees weekly, while adapting to a higher or lower frequency as needed. We also check up on mentors for a few minutes through Skype every two to four weeks.
What happens when I am matched with a mentee?
By signing up as a mentor, you agree to connect with a mentee when your situations and experiences are relevant. We will let you know when you are a potential match, then let you know if the mentee would like to connect. From there, we will provide a Skype training session and introduce you to the mentee.
Who can become a mentor?
Anyone who has left the closet and would like to help others in a similar situation is encouraged to sign up as a mentor. Mentors should show maturity and empathy. Please note, we conduct background checks to ensure the safety of our mentees. Although we currently only work in the United States, we encourage international people to sign up as potential mentors for the future when we expand.
How will I be supported?
We begin each mentorship with a Skype training session. Additionally, we check up for a few minutes every two to four weeks on Skype to make sure that you feel comfortable and prepared. We encourage mentors to reach out to us if they are ever looking for further support.
